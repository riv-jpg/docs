A great glove doesn't just look good — it changes how your player moves on the field. Gloves provide passive fielding stat bonuses that stack on top of your base attributes, influencing how often you snag line drives, scoop grounders in the hole, and gun down runners from the outfield wall. On top of the stat impact, every glove is rendered in full detail during gameplay: you'll see the web style, stitching color, and pocket depth on your player's hand every time they make a play.

## Position Recommendations

<Tip>
  Match your glove to your **primary position** before anything else. A shortstop and a center fielder need very different things from their glove — investing in the right stat bonuses for your role makes a much bigger impact than simply buying the most expensive option.
</Tip>

<Accordion title="Infielders (2B, SS, 3B) — Prioritize Hands and Accuracy">
  The middle and hot corners demand quick transfers and clean footwork. You need a glove that makes soft hands feel automatic and keeps your throws on target under pressure.

  **Recommended:** Trap-Eze Pro or Elite Fielder

  The Trap-Eze Pro's Accuracy and Arm Strength combo is great for shortstops with range to burn. Upgrade to the Elite Fielder when you're ready to cut your error rate in Story Mode's playoff pressure games.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Outfielders (LF, CF, RF) — Prioritize Range and Arm Strength">
  Cover ground, track fly balls into the gap, and have enough arm to keep runners honest. Center fielders especially need every point of Range they can get.

  **Recommended:** Elite Fielder or Gold Glove Series

  The Gold Glove Series' +9 Range and +5 Arm Strength are the gold standard for outfielders who want to be a weapon in right field or a vacuum in center. The special catch animation is a bonus that makes highlight-reel plays even more satisfying.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Catchers — Prioritize Hands and Arm Strength">
  Framing pitches, blocking balls in the dirt, and throwing out base-stealers all run through the same two stats. A catcher with elite Hands and Arm Strength changes the entire rhythm of the game.

  **Recommended:** Elite Fielder or Gold Glove Series

  The Elite Fielder's error rate reduction is especially valuable for catchers blocking low pitches. Graduate to the Gold Glove Series when your arm strength needs to truly punish stolen base attempts.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="First Basemen (1B) — Prioritize Hands">
  Scooping throws from all angles is the primary job at first base. You don't need the biggest arm — you need the softest hands in the infield.

  **Recommended:** Any Rare tier or above

  The Trap-Eze Pro is a cost-effective sweet spot for first basemen. If you're flush with tokens, the Elite Fielder's Hands bonus and error reduction will save you crucial outs across a full Story Mode season.
</Accordion>

## How to Purchase and Equip a Glove

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Shop">
    From the main menu, tap the **Shop** card to enter the store.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse the Gloves Section">
    Tap **Gloves** in the shop navigation. Tap any glove to open the **3D preview** — rotate it to inspect the web style, stitching, and pocket design. Stat bonuses and token cost are displayed alongside the model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Purchase the Glove">
    Tap **Purchase** and confirm your token spend. The glove is added to your inventory instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Equip and Hit the Field">
    Your new glove is **auto-equipped** to your player after purchase — jump straight into a game and you'll see it on your fielding hand immediately. Swap gloves anytime in **Player Editor → Gear tab**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You can own multiple gloves and switch between them freely before any match. Consider keeping a Hands-focused glove for Story Mode infield starts and a Range-focused glove for outfield assignments.
</Note>
