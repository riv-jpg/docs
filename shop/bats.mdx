Every bat in Stubly Baseball is more than a cosmetic — it's a piece of equipment that quietly shapes every at-bat you take. Passive stat bonuses stack on top of your base player stats, so the right bat can push a gap hit over the wall or give a speedy contact hitter that extra edge on the base paths. Browse the shop, preview each bat in full 3D, and unlock the one that matches how you play.

## Bat Tiers

## Featured Bats & Limited Editions

<Tip>
  Check the Shop every Monday — the **weekly featured bat** rotates in with a **15% token discount**, which can save you hundreds of tokens on higher-tier bats. If you've been eyeing a Composite or Carbon Pro, patience pays off.
</Tip>

Beyond the weekly rotation, keep an eye out for **limited-edition bats** that appear during seasonal events. The Championship Series event, for example, features exclusive bats with unique visual designs that aren't available at any other time of year. These don't offer stat advantages over standard tiers, but they're a mark of prestige in online multiplayer.

## How to Purchase and Equip a Bat

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Shop">
    From the main menu, tap the **Shop** card. You can also jump straight to it via the **Shop shortcut** in the Player Editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Browse the Bats Section">
    Tap **Bats** in the shop navigation. Tap any bat to load its **3D model viewer** — rotate the bat, inspect its design, and review its full stat bonuses and token cost side by side.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Purchase the Bat">
    Tap **Purchase** and confirm the transaction. The token cost is deducted from your balance immediately. The bat is added to your inventory — you'll see a confirmation animation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Equip and Play">
    Your new bat is **auto-equipped** after purchase so you can jump straight into a match. To swap bats, go to **Player Editor → Gear tab** and select from your inventory at any time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You can own multiple bats and swap between them freely before any match. Trying a different bat for Story Mode versus Quick Play is a great way to tune your loadout for each format.
</Note>
