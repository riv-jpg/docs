Fielding activates the moment the ball is put in play. You take direct control of your created player's position on the field, reading the ball off the bat and reacting in real time. Great defense isn't just about reflexes — it's about positioning before the pitch, knowing which catch type to attempt, and making clean, decisive throws before runners can advance.

## Movement and Positioning

When the ball is hit in your direction, the camera switches to your player's on-field perspective. Your first priority is getting into position before the ball arrives.

- Use the **joystick** (mobile/gamepad) or **WASD** (keyboard) to sprint toward the ball
- A **targeting circle** appears on the ground showing the ball's predicted landing spot — move to overlap it before the ball descends
- The earlier you reach the target circle, the more time you have to set your feet and make a clean catch

Your **Range stat** determines how far you can move from your starting position and still successfully catch the ball. A high Range stat lets you cover more ground, turning would-be hits into outs at the edges of your zone.

<Note>
  Equipping fielding-focused gear from the in-game Shop can boost your Range and Hands stats before a game. Check the Shop regularly for defensive equipment upgrades — even a small Range boost can turn a gapper into an out in tight situations.
</Note>

## Catch Types

Different ball trajectories call for different catch techniques. Read the ball early and choose the right catch type for each situation.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Routine Catch" icon="circle-check">
    Stand under the ball and hold the **Catch button** as it arrives. This is the standard catch for any ball hit directly to you. Your **Hands stat** determines your success rate — higher Hands means fewer bobbles on routine plays.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Running Catch" icon="person-running">
    Sprint to the ball's landing spot and tap the **Catch button** as you arrive. Running catches introduce a slight error risk, especially if you arrive at a sprint without time to set your feet. Improve your **Range stat** to reduce that risk by giving you more time to slow down before the ball lands.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Diving Catch" icon="arrow-down-to-line">
    When the ball is barely out of reach, double-tap the **Dive button** to send your player into a full extension dive. Diving catches are high risk, high reward — your **Range and Hands stats both factor into the success calculation**. A failed dive leaves you on the ground and the ball rolling away.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Jump Catch (Outfield)" icon="arrow-up">
    At the warning track or wall, tap the **Jump button + Catch button** simultaneously to leap and snag a potential home run. Timing is critical — jump too early and you miss the wall; jump too late and the ball clears your glove. This is the flashiest play in Stubly Baseball.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  When the targeting circle turns **yellow**, the ball is reachable but close — commit to a running catch and you'll likely make it. When it turns **red**, the ball is at the edge or beyond your Range. Attempt a dive only if the situation demands it (e.g., you're up by one run in the final inning). In lower-stakes moments, let the ball drop and hold the runner to a single rather than risking a diving miss that turns a single into a triple.
</Tip>

## Throwing

After making a catch, the throw interface appears immediately. Act quickly — every fraction of a second you hold the ball is a step the runner gains toward the next base.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify Your Target Base">
    Glance at the base indicators that light up on your HUD after the catch. The game highlights the optimal throw based on the game situation — but you can override it by tapping any base icon directly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose Throw Power">
    - **Tap quickly** — faster release, lower power; use for close plays at a nearby base
    - **Hold briefly** — stronger throw using full **Arm Strength**; use for long throws from the outfield or across the diamond
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hit the Green Zone">
    A throw accuracy meter appears as you hold the throw button. Release in the **green zone** for an on-target throw. Your **Accuracy stat** narrows the miss zone, giving you a larger window to release cleanly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Watch the Throw Land">
    A well-timed, accurate throw arrives at the base on a tight line. The baseman catches it and applies the tag or steps on the bag for a force out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Double Plays

The double play is the pitcher's best friend and the defense's most momentum-swinging play. To set up and execute a double play:

1. **Get ahead in the count** while pitching to induce a ground ball — sinkers and cutters low in the zone are your best tools
2. **Field the grounder cleanly** at second base or shortstop
3. **Tap the double play sequence button** (labeled **2B → 1B**) to initiate the pivot throw
4. A **pivot timing mini-game** activates — tap the throw button when the indicator hits the target zone to complete the throw before the runner arrives at first

<Warning>
  If you rush the pivot throw before the timing window reaches the green zone, the throw sails wide of first base — turning a potential double play into a throwing error and possibly allowing the go-ahead run to score. Take a split second to set the throw, even under pressure.
</Warning>

Double plays are most likely when:
- A runner is on first base with fewer than two outs
- The batter has a low Vision stat and is likely to chase low pitches
- The infield is playing at normal depth (not shifted or drawn in)

## Errors and Bobbles

Errors are a real risk in Stubly Baseball and directly impact runs allowed. Two types of errors can occur in the field:

**Bobbles (Fielding Errors)**
A low **Hands stat** or a mistimed Catch button press causes the ball to pop out of your glove. When a bobble occurs, your player reaches down to recover the ball — costing precious seconds and allowing runners to advance. Upgrade your Hands stat to reduce bobble frequency on routine and running catches.

**Throwing Errors**
Releasing your throw outside the green zone, or throwing before the accuracy meter fills, sends the ball off-target — pulling the baseman off the bag or sailing into the stands. A throwing error puts runners in scoring position and can break open a close game. Upgrade your **Accuracy stat** and avoid rushing throws to keep the ball on target.

<Note>
  Both error types are more likely under fatigue conditions. If your fielder has played deep into a long game in Story Mode, the Hands and Accuracy stats may degrade slightly. Substitute a fresh fielder from the bench when defensive precision is critical.
</Note>
