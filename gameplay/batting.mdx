Batting is the core offensive mechanic in Stubly Baseball. Your job at the plate is to read the incoming pitch, recognize its type and trajectory, and time your swing to make solid contact. The better your timing and pitch recognition, the harder and more precisely you hit — turning routine at-bats into scoring opportunities and solo shots into momentum-shifting home runs.

## The Batting Interface

When you step into the batter's box, the camera locks into a pitcher's-eye view. Three key elements overlay the screen:

- **Strike Zone Grid** — a transparent box marking the legal strike zone in front of the plate
- **Pitch Indicator Arc** — a brief trajectory curve that appears at the pitcher's release point, showing the ball's projected path
- **Swing Timing Meter** — a shrinking circular ring that closes in on the ball as it travels toward you

## The Timing Window

Nailing your timing is the single most important skill at the plate. Here's how it works:

1. The pitcher releases the ball and a pitch trajectory arc flashes briefly on screen.
2. A circular timing ring shrinks toward the incoming ball as it approaches the plate.
3. Tap the screen (or press your swing button) the moment the ring closes tightly around the ball.
4. Your contact quality is graded instantly based on how precisely you timed the tap.

| Zone | Color | Result |
|------|-------|--------|
| Perfect | 🟢 Green | Maximum power and contact; best exit velocity |
| Good | 🟡 Yellow | Solid contact; moderate power |
| Early / Late | 🟠 Orange | Weak contact; pull-side or opposite-field foul risk |
| Miss | 🔴 Red | Swing and miss; strike recorded |

<Note>
  Your batter's **Vision stat** directly widens the green Perfect timing window. Investing in Vision through the Upgrades screen gives you more margin for error on every swing — especially useful against high-velocity fastballs and late-breaking sliders.
</Note>

## Swing Types

Choose the right swing type for each situation. Each swing trades off contact, power, and speed differently.

<Accordion title="Normal Swing — Balanced Contact and Power">
  The default swing for most at-bats. A Normal Swing gives you a balanced mix of contact rate and hitting power, making it the safest choice in full counts or when you're unsure of the incoming pitch type.

  **How to trigger:** Tap the screen or press **A (Xbox) / Cross (PS)**.

  Use this swing when you want consistent, reliable contact without sacrificing too much power.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Power Swing — Maximum Distance, Higher Risk">
  Wind up for a full rip. The Power Swing loads your batter into a deeper stride, generating significantly more bat speed and exit velocity — at the cost of contact rate and swing timing window.

  **How to trigger:** Hold the swing button for **0.3 seconds** before releasing.

  Power Swings are most effective when:
  - You've identified the pitch type early (high Vision stat helps here)
  - The pitch is in your batter's hot zone
  - You're ahead in the count and can afford a miss

  <Warning>
    Power Swings noticeably increase your strikeout risk. Avoid them on two-strike counts unless you're confident in the pitch read.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Slap Hit — Speed Over Power">
  A quick, compact swing designed to drive the ball through the infield gaps at high speed. The Slap Hit sacrifices power almost entirely in exchange for a fast ball exit and a lower launch angle — making it ideal for contact hitters trying to beat the shift or set up a stolen base.

  **How to trigger:** Swipe quickly left or right (mobile), or press **S + A** (keyboard).

  Best used when:
  - You have a fast runner on base and want to manufacture a run
  - The infield is shifted and you want to hit to the open side
  - You're a contact-build batter with high Speed and low Power stats
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Bunt — Place the Ball Precisely">
  A soft, controlled tap that drops the ball in front of home plate. Bunts don't register as swings in the timing system — instead, you direct the ball's placement by holding the bunt icon before the pitch arrives.

  **How to trigger:** Tap the **Bunt icon** on the batting HUD before the pitcher releases the ball.

  Bunts are situational tools:
  - Sacrifice bunt to advance a runner from first to second
  - Bunt for a hit when the corners are playing deep
  - Drag bunt against an overshift to exploit the open third-base side
</Accordion>

## Reading Pitches

The more information you gather before the ball leaves the pitcher's hand, the better your swing decision will be.

- **Watch the release point and arm angle.** A high, over-the-top arm slot typically signals a fastball or curveball; a three-quarter slot is common for sliders and sinkers.
- **Curveballs and sliders break late.** Let the ball travel deeper into the zone before committing to your swing. Bailing out early turns breaking balls into easy strikeouts.
- **Changeups are deceptive by design.** The pitcher's arm speed looks identical to a fastball, but the ball arrives significantly slower. This mismatch is the most common cause of early swings — train yourself to wait longer before triggering your swing.

<Tip>
  Upgrade your **Vision stat** to unlock a brief pitch-type indicator icon that appears just before the ball leaves the pitcher's hand. Even a fraction of a second of advance knowledge is enough to stay back on breaking balls and attack fastballs with confidence.
</Tip>

## Spray Chart and Direction Control

You control where the ball goes when you make contact. Before the pitch is released, aim your swing to pull, go up the middle, or push it to the opposite field.

- **Mobile:** Swipe left or right on the directional joystick before the pitch
- **Gamepad:** Tilt the left stick left (pull) or right (opposite field) before swinging
- **Keyboard:** Hold the Left or Right arrow key before pressing your swing key

| Direction | Trade-off |
|-----------|-----------|
| Pull | Higher power, more home run potential, more strikeout risk against inside pitches |
| Center | Balanced contact and gap-shot probability |
| Opposite Field | Higher contact rate, lower power; effective against outside pitches |

## Home Run Mechanics

Home runs are the most satisfying outcome in Stubly Baseball — and they're never accidental. To maximize your home run probability:

1. Identify a hittable pitch in your batter's **hot zone** (shown on the zone grid before each at-bat)
2. Aim your swing toward your pull side for maximum launch power
3. Execute a **Power Swing** with **Perfect timing**

When all three conditions align, the ball launches off the bat with a thunderous crack and the camera cuts to a **cinematic slow-motion sequence** as the ball arcs over the wall — complete with crowd reactions and a full stadium fly-through shot.

<Note>
  Your batter's **Power stat** determines the raw distance of home run shots. Even a Perfect-timed Power Swing may result in a warning-track out if your Power stat is low. Upgrade Power in the player stat screen to turn deep flies into home runs consistently.
</Note>
