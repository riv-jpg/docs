The first thing you see after logging in is the Stubly Baseball main menu — a cinematic 3D space where large, swipeable cards float in front of a live stadium backdrop. Ambient crowd noise drifts in from the stands, dynamic lighting shifts with the in-game time of day, and your active player stands ready at the center. Everything you need is one swipe or tap away.

## The Five Main Cards

Each card in the main menu represents a core area of the game. Swipe left or right to move between them, or tap the navigation dots at the bottom of the screen to jump directly to a specific card.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Player Card" icon="user" href="/player/player-editor">
    Displays your active player as a real-time 3D preview alongside your current stats and equipped gear. Tap the card to open the **Player Editor**, where you can upgrade stats, swap equipment, and customize your look.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Quick Play" icon="bolt" href="/multiplayer/overview">
    Launches online multiplayer matchmaking instantly. The card shows your current **win/loss record** and **rank badge** so you always know where you stand before queuing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Batting Cages" icon="baseball-bat-ball" href="/modes/batting-cages">
    Opens solo practice mode so you can sharpen your swing without any pressure. The card displays your **batting average** and a summary of stats from your most recent session.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Story Mode" icon="book-open" href="/modes/story-mode">
    Takes you into the narrative campaign. The card shows your **current season**, **active chapter**, and a **progression bar** so you can see exactly how far you are through the story.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Shop" icon="store" href="/shop/tokens">
    Opens the in-game shop, where you can browse and purchase bats, gloves, uniforms, and token bundles. Your **current token balance** is displayed directly on the card before you tap in.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## The Top Bar

The top edge of the main menu always stays visible no matter which card is in focus. It contains four elements:

| Element | What It Shows |
|---|---|
| **Username** | Your display name and current level badge |
| **Token Balance** | Your spendable token total, updated in real time |
| **Notification Bell** | New rewards, messages, and game announcements |
| **Settings Gear** | Opens the Settings menu for account, audio, graphics, and controls |

Tap the notification bell whenever the icon shows a badge to collect any pending rewards or read important announcements from the Stubly team.

## Navigating Between Cards

<Steps>
  <Step title="Swipe to browse">
    Swipe **left** or **right** anywhere on the screen to move through the five cards one at a time. Each card snaps smoothly into place at the center.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Jump to a specific card">
    Tap any of the **five dots** at the bottom of the screen to jump directly to the corresponding card without swiping through the others.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pin a default card">
    **Long-press** any card to get the option to pin it as your **default opening screen**. The next time you launch the game, that card loads front and center automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  The Player Card updates in real time. Equip a new bat in the Player Editor, return to the main menu, and you will see your player holding it immediately — no restart or reload required.
</Tip>

## Quick Reference

<Accordion title="What does the rank badge on Quick Play mean?">
  Your rank badge reflects your current standing in the competitive matchmaking system. Ranks run from **Rookie** through **All-Star** and update after every ranked match. Hover over or long-press the badge on the Quick Play card for a breakdown of your current rank score and the threshold for the next rank.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I access the Player Editor from anywhere in the menu?">
  Yes. Tap the **Player Card** to go directly to the Player Editor from the main menu. You can also reach it during a Story Mode session via the pause menu, or between innings in Quick Play.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why does the stadium backdrop look different each time I log in?">
  The stadium backdrop uses **dynamic lighting** that mirrors a real day/night cycle tied to your local time zone. Log in at night and the stadium lights blaze against a dark sky; log in at noon and sunlight streams across the outfield. Weather effects also appear occasionally based on regional event schedules.
</Accordion>

<Warning>
  If the main menu cards appear blank or the 3D visuals fail to load, your device may have lost its internet connection. Stubly Baseball requires a connection to render live stadium data and sync your player preview. Check your connection and relaunch the game.
</Warning>
