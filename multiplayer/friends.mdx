The Friends system in Stubly Baseball keeps you connected to the players you care about most. You can see who's online, peek at their stats and gear, and fire off a direct challenge without waiting in matchmaking — turning every session into an opportunity to settle the score with your crew.

## Accessing Your Friends List

Open the **Main Menu** and tap the **person icon** in the top navigation bar to open your Friends list. Your full friend roster, online statuses, and pending requests all live here.

## Adding a Friend

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tap 'Add Friend'">
    On the Friends screen, tap the **+ icon** (Add Friend) in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Their Username or Friend Code">
    Type the other player's exact Stubly username — the search is case-insensitive, so capitalization doesn't matter. Alternatively, share your own **Friend Code** (a short alphanumeric code unique to your account) and have them enter it instead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for Acceptance">
    The other player receives a friend request notification. Once they accept, you both appear on each other's friends lists immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You can also add a friend straight from the post-match screen. After any Quick Play or Ranked game, tap your opponent's name and select **Add Friend** — no need to know their username in advance.
</Note>

## What You Can See on Your Friends List

Each friend card shows you everything you need to know at a glance:

- **Online status** — a green dot means Online, an orange baseball icon means In Match, and a grey dot means Offline.
- **Ranked tier badge** — their current competitive tier so you can size up the competition before challenging.
- **Last season's record** — their win/loss totals from the previous season give you a sense of their experience level.

Tap any friend's profile card to open their full profile, including detailed stats, equipped gear, and an interactive 3D preview of their player.

## Challenging a Friend to a Match

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find an Online Friend">
    On your Friends list, locate a friend showing a green **Online** status dot.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send a Match Invite">
    Tap their card, then select **Challenge to Private Match**. This sends them a direct match invite.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for Acceptance">
    Your friend has **60 seconds** to accept the invite. Once they accept, you both enter the Private Match lobby with you as the host.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  As the host, you control stadium selection, game settings, and when the match begins. Check out the Private Matches guide for a full breakdown of host options.
</Tip>

## Removing and Blocking Players

Use these options to manage who can interact with you:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Remove a Friend">
    Swipe left on any friend's card and tap **Remove Friend**. This removes them from your list and removes you from theirs. They can still send you a new friend request unless you block them.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Block a Player">
    Swipe left on a friend's card and tap **Block**. Blocked players cannot send you friend requests, match invites, or any in-game messages.

    <Warning>
      Blocking is permanent until you manually unblock the player. To unblock someone, navigate to **Settings → Privacy → Blocked Players** and tap **Unblock** next to their name.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
