Ranked Play is Stubly Baseball's competitive mode where every match has real stakes. Win and you earn Rank Points (RP) that push you up the ladder; lose and you give some back. Six tiers separate casual contenders from the game's elite, and a seasonal structure keeps the competition fresh with resets, rewards, and a chance to prove yourself again each cycle.

## RP Gains and Losses

Every ranked match adjusts your RP total based on the outcome and how well you played:

| Outcome | RP Change |
|---|---|
| **Win** | +25 base RP, up to +15 performance bonus |
| **Loss** | −20 base RP |
| **Close loss** (decided by 1 run) | −15 RP (−5 reduction applied) |
| **Abandon / disconnect** | −30 RP |

The **performance bonus** on wins rewards you for dominant play — runs scored, strikeouts thrown, and standout defensive plays all contribute to a bonus between +0 and +15 RP added on top of the base gain.

<Note>
  At **Platinum and above**, demotion protection prevents your RP from falling below the floor of your current tier for the **first 3 games after promotion**. Use that window to stabilize before the safety net lifts.
</Note>

## Gear Stat Caps in Ranked

<Accordion title="Why gear is capped — and what the limits are">
  Ranked Play enforces stat caps on gear bonuses to keep the ladder skill-based rather than gear-dependent. Here's how caps work:

  - Gear stat bonuses are capped at **+5 total per stat** in all Ranked matches.
  - In Quick Play and Story Mode, your full gear bonus applies with no cap.
  - Your **base player stats** — earned through the stat upgrade system — are **always uncapped** in Ranked. Investing tokens into stat upgrades is the highest-value path to Ranked performance.
  - If your equipped gear exceeds the +5 cap on any stat, the surplus is silently trimmed for the duration of the Ranked match. Your gear is not modified permanently.

  **Example:** If your bat gives +8 Power, only +5 Power applies in a Ranked match. The remaining +3 is ignored until you return to Quick Play or Story Mode.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Which stats are affected by the cap?">
  The +5 cap applies to all gear-derived bonuses across every stat category, including:

  - **Power** (bat contact and home run distance)
  - **Speed** (baserunning and fielding range)
  - **Accuracy** (pitch placement and throw precision)
  - **Stamina** (pitcher fatigue rate and batter consistency late in at-bats)
  - **Reaction** (batter timing window and fielder jump reads)
</Accordion>

## Placement Matches

If you're new to Ranked or returning after a seasonal reset, you play **5 placement matches** before the system assigns you a starting rank. Placement results are used to estimate your skill level and typically place you **one tier below your calibrated level** — giving you an achievable climb rather than dropping you directly into unfamiliar territory.

<Tip>
  Play your placement matches when you're sharp and unhurried. Your placement rank sets the ceiling you'll need to push past early in the season, so a focused placement run saves you grind time later.
</Tip>

## Seasonal Resets and Rewards

Ranked seasons run for approximately **8 weeks**. At season end, the ladder soft-resets to redistribute players and create fresh competition:

| End-of-Season Rank | Reset Destination |
|---|---|
| Legend | Platinum |
| Diamond | Gold |
| Platinum | Silver |
| Gold | Bronze |
| Silver / Bronze | Bronze |

### Season-End Rewards

Finish a season strong to earn exclusive cosmetics that can't be obtained anywhere else:

- **Gold or above** — Unique bat skin + glove skin for that season
- **Legend** — All Gold+ rewards plus an exclusive **animated bat trail** available only to Legend finishers

<Warning>
  Season rewards are distributed within **72 hours** of the season ending. Make sure your Stubly account has a verified email address so your rewards aren't lost. Check **Profile → Account Settings** to confirm your email.
</Warning>
