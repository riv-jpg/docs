Every number on your player card means something on the field. Stubly Baseball tracks fifteen distinct stats across three categories — Batting, Pitching, and Fielding — and each one shapes how your player performs in real time. Understanding what drives each stat helps you make smarter upgrade decisions and build a player that fits your playstyle.

## Stat Basics

All stats use a scale of **1 to 99**. When you create a new player, the game assigns a starting stat profile based on the **primary position** you choose in the Player Editor. A new Pitcher, for example, begins with elevated Velocity and Control but modest Batting stats, while a new Shortstop starts with balanced Fielding and Speed.

<Note>
  Your primary position determines your **starting stat weights** — the positions that matter most to your role get higher base values at creation. You can still upgrade any stat regardless of position, but your starting point reflects the role you chose. Change your primary position in the [Player Editor](/player/player-editor) at any time; it does not retroactively alter your existing stat values.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Resist the urge to spread upgrades evenly across all fifteen stats. Identify the four or five stats that matter most to your position and push those into the 70s before touching anything else. A well-rounded player at 55 across the board is outperformed by a specialist at 80 where it counts.
</Tip>

## Stat Categories

<Accordion title="Batting Stats">

  Batting stats govern everything that happens from the moment the pitch leaves the pitcher's hand to the moment the ball lands — or you walk back to the dugout.

  | Stat | Range | What It Does |
  |---|---|---|
  | Contact | 1–99 | How consistently you make contact with the ball. High Contact shrinks the strikeout window and keeps your swing on plane longer. |
  | Power | 1–99 | Determines maximum hit distance and home run potential. Higher Power means more fly balls carry over the wall and hard-hit line drives travel further into the gap. |
  | Speed | 1–99 | Your base running speed around the bases and your success rate on stolen-base attempts. High Speed also improves first-to-third and tag-up reads. |
  | Vision | 1–99 | Pitch recognition ability. High Vision widens the strike-zone read window, giving you more time to identify pitch type and location before you must commit to a swing. |
  | Composure | 1–99 | How steadily your player performs under pressure — full counts, late innings, and playoff scenarios. Low Composure triggers a mild penalty to Contact and Vision in high-stress at-bats. |
  | Clutch | 1–99 | A bonus applied to both Contact and Power during high-leverage moments: runners in scoring position (RISP), tie games in the seventh inning or later, and walk-off situations. |

  **Which positions value Batting stats most:** All positions use Batting stats when at the plate, but Corner Outfielders (LF/RF), First Basemen, and Designated Hitters benefit most from high Power and Clutch. Middle infielders and Catchers typically prioritize Speed and Contact over raw Power.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Pitching Stats">

  Pitching stats determine how effectively you retire batters when you take the mound. Even if your primary position is a fielding role, Story Mode may call on you to pitch in a pinch — so it's worth knowing what each stat does.

  | Stat | Range | What It Does |
  |---|---|---|
  | Velocity | 1–99 | Your fastball speed in mph. Higher Velocity compresses the batter's reaction window, making your heater harder to square up. Each point above 60 adds roughly 0.5 mph to your four-seamer. |
  | Control | 1–99 | How tightly your pitched balls land inside the targeted zone. Low Control causes pitches to miss the catcher's glove by a wider margin, resulting in more balls, hit batters, and wild pitches. |
  | Break | 1–99 | The sharpness and depth of breaking ball movement on curveballs, sliders, and changeups. High Break produces late, dramatic movement that makes off-speed pitches difficult to lay off. |
  | Stamina | 1–99 | How many innings and pitches you can throw before Velocity and Control begin to degrade. Once your Stamina bar empties, both stats drop steadily until the end of the inning. |
  | Composure | 1–99 | Your resistance to giving up runs in high-pressure pitching situations — bases loaded, late-inning leads, and playoff games. A low Composure pitcher becomes measurably less accurate and loses break sharpness when stressed. |

  **Which positions value Pitching stats most:** Pitchers, obviously, but relief pitchers benefit from maximizing Velocity and Break over Stamina. Starting pitchers need all five stats, with Stamina being the priority for deep outings.

  <Note>
    Pitching Composure and Batting Composure are **separate stats** that share a name. Upgrading one does not affect the other. The Upgrade screen labels them **"Composure (Pitch)"** and **"Composure (Bat)"** to avoid confusion.
  </Note>

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Fielding Stats">

  Fielding stats govern every defensive play — from range on ground balls to the strength and accuracy of your throws across the diamond.

  | Stat | Range | What It Does |
  |---|---|---|
  | Arm Strength | 1–99 | The speed and distance of your throws. Critical for Outfielders cutting down runners at home and for Catchers throwing to second on stolen-base attempts. Low Arm Strength causes throws to sail short or arrive too late. |
  | Accuracy | 1–99 | The precision of your throws. Low Accuracy increases the chance of wild throws, pulling fielders off the bag and turning routine outs into errors and extra bases. |
  | Range | 1–99 | How much ground you cover in the field. High Range widens the dive and lunge window, letting you reach balls that a lower-range player can only watch hit the turf. |
  | Hands | 1–99 | Your glove reliability on received balls. High Hands reduces the error rate on ground balls, line drives, and short hops. Low Hands causes bobbles that turn hits into extra bases or errors on plays you reach. |

  **Which positions value Fielding stats most:**

  - **Catchers** — Arm Strength and Hands are paramount
  - **Middle Infielders (2B, SS)** — Range and Accuracy on double-play turns
  - **Third Basemen** — Hands and Arm Strength for hard-hit rockets
  - **Center Fielders** — Range above all else
  - **Corner Outfielders** — Arm Strength to keep runners honest

</Accordion>

## Gear Bonuses

Your base stats are only part of the picture. Bats and gloves purchased or earned through the [Shop](/shop) apply additional **stat bonuses on top of your base values**. A bat might add +4 Power and +2 Contact; a premium fielding glove might add +5 Range and +3 Hands. Gear bonuses are additive and do not count toward your upgrade cost tiers.

See [Upgrading Stats](/player/upgrading-stats) to learn how to spend tokens on permanent base stat improvements.
