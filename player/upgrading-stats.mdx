Every great player in Stubly Baseball is built one upgrade at a time. Tokens are the currency you spend to raise your base stats permanently — no match-by-match buffs, no temporary boosts. When you upgrade a stat, that number goes up for good, and your player carries it into every game mode from that point forward.

## What Are Tokens?

Tokens are earned by playing games across all modes — Multiplayer, Story Mode, and Batting Cages each award tokens on completion. To see exactly how to earn more, open **Shop → Tokens** from the main menu.

<Note>
  Tokens are separate from the in-game currency you spend on gear in the Shop. You cannot buy tokens with real money — they are earned exclusively through gameplay.
</Note>

## Opening the Upgrade Menu

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to your Player Card">
    From the main menu, tap your **Player Card** in the upper-left corner to open the player overview screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap the Upgrade button">
    Tap the **Upgrade** button (star icon) beneath your overall rating. This opens the Upgrade screen, which is separate from the Player Editor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your stats">
    The Upgrade screen displays all fifteen stats as horizontal bars. Each bar shows your **current value**, a marker at **99** (the max), and the **token cost** to add one point at your current level.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a stat to upgrade">
    Tap any stat bar. A detail panel slides up from the bottom of the screen showing the stat's current value, a plain-language description of what it does, and the exact token cost for the next point.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Queue your upgrade">
    Tap **+1** to add a single point to your queue, or drag the **slider** to queue multiple points at once. The panel updates the total token cost in real time as you adjust.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm and apply">
    Tap **Confirm Upgrade** to spend your tokens and apply the change immediately. Your stat bar updates on screen right away. The upgrade takes effect in your **next match** — it does not alter the stats of any match currently in progress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Token Cost Curve

Upgrading a stat gets progressively more expensive the higher it climbs. Plan your spending with the full cost curve in mind:

| Stat Range | Cost Per Point |
|---|---|
| 1 – 40 | 50 tokens |
| 41 – 60 | 100 tokens |
| 61 – 75 | 200 tokens |
| 76 – 85 | 400 tokens |
| 86 – 95 | 750 tokens |
| 96 – 99 | 1,500 tokens |

**Example:** Raising Contact from 55 to 65 costs 6 points in the 41–60 range (600 tokens) plus 4 points in the 61–75 range (800 tokens) — **1,400 tokens total**.

<Warning>
  There is **no stat reset (respec)** in Stubly Baseball. Once you spend tokens on a stat, those points are permanent and cannot be moved to a different stat. Plan your upgrade path carefully before you confirm any spend — especially once you reach the expensive 76+ tiers.
</Warning>

## Upgrade Strategy

<Tip>
  Focus your first wave of upgrades on the two or three stats that matter most for your primary position. Getting a core stat from 50 to 70 costs far fewer tokens than pushing it from 70 to 90, and the gameplay impact is immediately noticeable.
</Tip>

<Accordion title="Position-based stat priorities">

  Use this guide as a starting point. Your playstyle may differ, but these priorities reflect the stats with the highest gameplay return per token spent for each position.

  | Position | Top Priority Stats |
  |---|---|
  | Pitcher | Velocity, Control, Break |
  | Catcher | Arm Strength, Hands, Composure (Bat) |
  | First Base | Power, Contact, Hands |
  | Second Base | Range, Accuracy, Speed |
  | Third Base | Hands, Arm Strength, Clutch |
  | Shortstop | Range, Accuracy, Speed |
  | Left Field | Power, Clutch, Arm Strength |
  | Center Field | Range, Speed, Contact |
  | Right Field | Arm Strength, Power, Clutch |

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Best early-game batting upgrades">

  If you play any hitting-heavy mode — including Story Mode or online multiplayer — prioritize **Contact** and **Vision** in your first upgrade sessions. These two stats give you the largest batting improvement for the fewest tokens because both start in the 1–40 range for most new players (50 tokens per point). A Contact score in the mid-60s dramatically reduces strikeouts, and a Vision score above 60 gives you enough pitch-read time to lay off breaking balls out of the zone.

  Push both to 60 before spending tokens on Power or Speed. The cost jumps at 61, so time your spending to get the maximum points at the lower rate.

</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Using gear to bridge stat gaps">

  You don't have to out-spend the upgrade curve to be competitive. **Bats and gloves from the Shop** provide flat stat bonuses on top of your base values — and those bonuses don't cost tokens. A bat that adds +6 Power and +4 Contact can carry you through a stat gap while you save tokens for the expensive 76–85 tier. Check the [Shop](/shop) regularly for gear that complements your current stat profile.

</Accordion>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="Can I upgrade stats for a secondary position?">
  All fifteen stats belong to your single player profile — there are no position-specific stat pools. Upgrading Velocity improves your pitching whether your primary position is Pitcher or First Base. There is no separate stat sheet for your secondary position.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do gear bonuses count toward upgrade cost tiers?">
  No. Gear bonuses are additive on top of your base stat but are invisible to the upgrade cost formula. If your base Contact is 55 and your bat adds +5, the game treats your Contact as **55** for upgrade cost purposes — you still pay the 41–60 rate for the next point.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="When does an upgrade take effect?">
  Immediately after you tap **Confirm Upgrade**, your stat bar reflects the new value. The upgrade applies to any match you start from that point forward. It does not alter stats mid-match if you upgrade during a pause or between innings in a live game.
</Accordion>
